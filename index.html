About the Workshop

Bridging Academic Research and Real-World Practice

The workshop "Theory and Practice of Sustainability: Convergences and Challenges in Europe" brings together researchers, policymakers, and practitioners to examine work at the interface between theory and practice in sustainability.

The event will open with a roundtable bringing together academics and professionals, aimed at discussing the gaps between sustainability concepts and their implementation in organizational and territorial contexts. In the afternoon, parallel academic sessions will host presentations and discussions of accepted papers.

This workshop seeks to foster interdisciplinary dialogue on investment decisions, market responses, governance mechanisms, and sustainability transitions, with particular attention to territorial and regional dynamics in Europe, including the Nouvelle-Aquitaine region.

Research Topics

We invite submissions on the following themes

Sectoral Perspectives

  • Industrial sectors and manufacturing activities
  • Agriculture and agri-food systems
  • Wine and viticulture industries
  • Sustainability reporting and governance
  • Territorial embeddedness and value creation

Institutions & Investment

  • Climate and environmental regulation
  • Policy uncertainty and capital allocation
  • Institutional quality and sustainability outcomes
  • Cross-country differences in sustainable finance

Information & Disclosure

  • ESG disclosure quality and transparency
  • Greenwashing and credibility issues
  • Market reactions to sustainability narratives
  • Media coverage and information processing

Corporate Strategy

  • Corporate sustainability strategies
  • Environmental performance and resilience
  • Green innovation and supply chains
  • Market perception and investment attractiveness

Technology & Data

  • AI, alternative data, and ESG measurement
  • Fintech and digital platforms
  • Algorithmic interpretation of climate risks
  • Sustainability assessment technologies

Behavioral & Social Dimensions

  • Investor beliefs and sustainability preferences
  • Household vs. institutional responses
  • Social norms and legitimacy
  • Sustainable investment behavior

Investment Outcomes

  • Portfolio diversification and risk management
  • Asset pricing under sustainability constraints
  • Volatility and downside risk
  • Liquidity and cost of capital

Submission Guidelines

How to Submit

Authors are invited to submit extended abstracts (up to 500 words) or full papers presenting ongoing research.

Language: English

Format: PDF

Send to: aammari@inseec.com

Special Roundtable Session

A dedicated roundtable will bring together academics and professionals (company representatives and practitioners) to discuss the bridge between sustainability theory and real-world practices, highlighting implementation challenges, practitioner feedback, and implications for academic research and public policy.

Awards

Outstanding papers will be selected for awards during the workshop. Recognition will be granted to selected papers that demonstrate exceptional contribution to sustainability research and practice.

Publication Opportunity

Special Issue Invitation

Special Issue – Bankers, Markets and Investors

A selection of high-quality papers presented at the workshop will be invited to submit to a Special Issue of the journal Bankers, Markets and Investors.

Special Issue Title: “Sustainability, Uncertainty, and Investment Decisions: Institutions, Information, and Market Responses”

All submissions will follow the journal’s standard peer-review process.
